Motor Vehicle Death Rates in the United States Across Age and Gender

By Abby Moulton

The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services conducted a research study to determine the rate of deaths by age and gender, per 100,000 population, for motor vehicle occupants killed in crashes in 2014. The data represents all 50 of the United States by categorizing occupants to male or female and in age brackets of 0-20, 21-34, 35-54, and 55+. Data was suppressed for a fatality rate of less than 20 deaths per 100,000 population.


All Ages in All States

The following map represents the rate of motor vehicle deaths for all ages in every state in 2014. The death rates have been categorized into five segments to display the variation in death rates across the country. Wyoming had the greatest death rate of 21 per 100,000 population while Rhode Island has the smallest rate of 2.3. In comparison, the national death rate for all ages in the United States was 6.8.
